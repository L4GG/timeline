Russian response to Obama’s Dec. 29, 2016, sanctions announcement was immediate. The Russian government made clear that Putin would retaliate in kind. The sanctions caused thirty-five Russian diplomats to be expelled from the U.S. and two Russian compounds were closed.

Putin's press secretary, Dmitry Peskovto, said, “There is no doubt that Russia’s adequate and mirror response will make Washington officials feel very uncomfortable, as well.” Russian Foreign minister Sergey Larov proposed that Putin “announce thirty-one personnel of the U.S. Embassy in Moscow and four diplomats from the Consulate General in St. Petersburg persona non grata,” as well as the closure of two facilities used by American diplomats.

Source: The New Yorker
