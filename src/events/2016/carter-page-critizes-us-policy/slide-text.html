Two weeks before the Republican National Convention, Trump foreign policy adviser Carter Page travels to Moscow and once again criticizes U.S. policy. Speaking at a commencement address for the New Economic School, an institution funded in part by major Russian oligarchs close to Putin, Page asserts that “Washington and other West capitals” had impeded progress in Russia “through their often hypocritical focus on ideas such as democratization, inequality, corruption and regime change.”

Source: Reuters
