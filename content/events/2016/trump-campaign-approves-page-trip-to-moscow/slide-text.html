Prior to his Moscow travels in July 2016, Carter Page asked his supervisor, Trump Campaign Director of National Security J.D. Gordon, for permission to take the trip. Gordon advised against it. Page then obtained formal approval via email from then-campaign manager Corey Lewandowski and spokesperson Hope Hicks. A statement from Hicks stated Page was not representing the campaign in Moscow. The campaign fired Lewandowski on June 20, prior to the trip. The campaign would later distance itself from Page.
