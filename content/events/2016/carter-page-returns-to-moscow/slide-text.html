Previous Trump foreign policy adviser Carter Page returns to Moscow, telling Russian state-run news agency RIA Novosti he will meet with "business leaders and thought leaders." Kremlin spokesperson Dmitri S. Peskov, the Kremlin’s spokesman, told news agency Interfax that government leaders had no plans to meet with Page and that they had never had any contact with him. Russia’s deputy foreign minister, Sergei Ryabkov, said his department had no plans to meet with Page.

Source: New York Times
