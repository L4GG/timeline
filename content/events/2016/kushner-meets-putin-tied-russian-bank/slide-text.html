Jared Kusher, Trump’s son-in-law, Senior White House Adviser, and head of Kushner Companies, meets with executives of Russian state-run development bank Vnesheconombank (VEB) in late 2016.

U.S. officials allege that Kushner first met with former National Security Adviser Michael Flynn and Russian Ambassador Sergey Kislyak, and then met with VEB chairman Sergei Gorkov in December 2016 during a bank roadshow. Gorkov was appointed head of the VEB by Russian president Vladimir Putin in early 2016 and is a decorated member of the Russian Federal Security Service. VEB, along with other Russian banks, has been under Western economic sanctions since 2014 concerning Russia’s annexation of part of Ukraine.

Kushner has agreed to testify before the U.S. Senate over suspected Russian interference in the presidential election.
