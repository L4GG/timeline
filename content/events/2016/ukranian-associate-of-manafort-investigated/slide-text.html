It is reported that Ukranian-Russian Konstantin Kilimnik, a long-time associate of former Trump campaign CEO Paul Manafort, is being investigated by the FBI and State Department in part because he may have influenced the GOP platform on Russia during the 2016 RNC Convention. Kilimnik made at least two trips to the U.S. in April and during the summer of 2016 and met with Manafort. Both U.S. and Ukraine officials suspect Kilimnik has ties to Russian intelligence, which he denies.

In 2005, Kilimnik was terminated from a decade-long role at the U.S.-based International Republican Institute in Moscow due to suspected Russian ties. He then was hired by Manafort as part of a private equity group that purchased a Ukraine-based cable and internet company from funds provided by Russian oligarch Oleg Deripaska. They also worked for Ukrainian oligarch Rinat Akhmetov's businesses, which later led to political work when Akhmetov began funding the campaign of former Ukrainian Prime Minister Viktor Yanukovych. Kilimnik continues to advise Opposition Bloc.
